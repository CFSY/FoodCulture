History

While the Chinese have been visiting India for millennia in search of Buddhist teachings, Yang Tai Chow was the first recorded Chinese to migrate to India for better material prospects. In 1778 he put down roots in Kolkata, or Calcutta, the then capital of British India and the easiest accessible metropolitan area from China by land.He helped create Hakka noodles with other several chefs


Instructions

1.Combine all ingredients of the “Sauce” in a food processor till it is almost like paste; if it gets too dry, add a few teaspoons of water, but do not make it too runny. Keep aside.

2.Heat about 20 cups of water (or enough for the noodles to be submerged under water). Bring it to a boil, add some salt and a splash of oil and cook for not more than 3 minutes. The noodles should be al dente; drain quickly and spread the noodles on a flat surface and splash some more oil to prevent them from sticking to each other.

3.Heat the sesame oil in a large wok. Add the chili flakes. and after half a minute add the sliced onions. Cook at high heat till the onions are just about clear. a Immediately add the processed ingredients for the “sauce” and the salt. Cook at high heat for about 3-5 minutes or till the raw taste of the garlic disappears. Lower the heat; add the vinegar and the soy sauce. Stir everything together till well combined.

4.Increase the heat to high, add the bell pepper, carrots, and beans and cook for only a couple of minutes. The vegetables should retain their color and crunch. Switch off the heat.

5.Add the noodles to the wok and stir them in carefully till all the cooked ingredients in the pan and the noodles are very well combined. Add the shredded cabbage.

6.Switch on the heat and cook the noodles on very high heat while flipping them every frequently, so the sauce, veggies and the noodles are combined uniformly while making sure the noodles do not stick at the bottom of the wok; cook for about 3-4 minutes. Switch off the heat.

7.Combine the chopped green onions and serve immediately with some Hot Oil.


Recipe

  • 1. 400 grams Hakka Noodles (I used Ching’s wheat 100% vegetarian noodles ) – The egg noodles work as well.
  • 2. 2 carrots, peeled and cut into matchsticks
  • 3. 1 bell pepper/capsicum, juliened
  • 4. 1/2 small green cabbage, shredded
  • 5. 3 cups or about 12 oz beans, cut diagonally or use the frozen french style cut beans
  • 6. 1 medium onion, sliced into thin half moons
  • 7. 1 bunch green onions/scallions, chopped (green and white parts)
  • 8. 1 tablespoon red chilli flakes
  • 9. 1/4 cup Asian Sesame Oil
  • 10. 4 tablespoons dark soy sauce (See Note below)
  • 11. 2 tablespoons vinegar
  • 12. salt
  • 13. about 6-8 cloves of garlic (when processed it should be about 1/6 cup)
  • 14. 3/4 inch piece fresh ginger, grated
  • 15. 2 tablespoons hot/chili sauce, like Sriracha or the Green Chili Sauce that we usually use in India
  • 16. a little less than 1/4 cup ketchup or tomato paste
  • 17. 3 teaspoons white sesame seeds
