Teochew

Characteristics

Characteristics

Of all the cuisines included here, Teochew is known to be the healthiest. It is particularly well known for its seafood and vegetarian dishes. Flavouring with sauces and condiments is minimal. Furthermore, as Teochew cuisine is known to be delicate, there is less oil used. The technique is very much emphasised in Teochew Cuisine. If the steaming, braising, poaching or frying is done to perfection, the end product will be great.

Kong bak Pau

Long before what we know as the hamburger in the West, came “Gua Bao,” A Chinese sandwich, also known as a Taiwanese Hamburger, made with a steamed white bread bun, sweet pork, pickled mustard, peanut powder, cilantro and coriander. The bun is allowed to cool after steaming, and is dry and fluffy when served.

Mui

It was found in China when an emperor ate congee with millet which then had many variations since then. There is Cantonese congee, Teochew congee, also known as Mui, and much more. Mui origin is unknown and is based on many historical accounts is served during times of famine in China.

Kueh Chap

A Teochew dish, Kueh Chap is origin from Chaozhou China. An acquired taste as the flat rice sheets, served in dark soy soup, are typically served with pig offal, especially intestines.
