Beef Brisket Noodles

  1. Home
  2. Beef Brisket Noodles

History

It was created by the Hui people ( a Chinese Muslim ethnic group) during the Tang dynasty of China.


Instructions

1. Bring a large pot of water to boil. Add the chunks of beef brisket and tendons. Bring to boil for 5 minutes to remove scums. Rinse with water and set in the slow cooker (or large dutch oven pot). In a smaller pot, stir fry minced shallots, garlic and ginger slices and the spices until aromatic. Add all the condiments and stir fry for a minute or two.

  2. Transfer the sauce mix to the brisket and tendons.  add the stock or water and bring it to a simmer. Cook for 2 hours. Add daikon cubes, and continue to simmer until brisket is fork tender (another 2 hours).

  3. You can serve immediately over steamed rice or Hong Kong egg noodles. Likewise with all stews, the flavors will intensify overnight which is best to eat the next day. 

Recipe

  • 1kg beef brisket, trim fat and cut into chucks
  • 500g beef tendon, cut into chunks
  • 500g daikon/white radish, peeled and cut into big cubes (optional)
  • 1 liter chicken stock or water enough to immerse all the ingredients
  • 100g old ginger, cut into thick slices
  • 5 small shallots or 2 larger shallots, finely minced
  • 4 cloves of garlic, finely minced
    •  

  • Aromatics

  • 1 teaspoon cardamon (about 5) 
  • 2 dried tangerine peel
  • 2 cinnamon stick
  • 5 star anise
  • ½ teaspoon white pepper
  • 3-4 bay leaf
    •  

  • Condiments

  • 100g Chu Hou sauce (Lee Kum Kee or Homemade)
  • 2 pieces of red fermented bean curd
  • 1 teaspoon five-spice powder
  • 2 heap tablespoon sugar
  • 100 ml yellow rice wine (can be replaced with shaoxing wine)
  • 1 tablespoon dark soy sauce
