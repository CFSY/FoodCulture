Nasi Kunyit

  1. Home
  2. Nasi Kunyit

History

Nasi Kunyit is rice dish that transcends two cultures in Malaysia; Chinese and Malay. For the Chinese, Nasi Kunyit is part and parcel of baby’s full moon


Instructions

1. Rinse rice under cold running water, then combine in a bowl with turmeric and enough water to cover. Soak for 8 hours or overnight.

2. Drain rice, then place in a steamer lined with muslin or a clean tea towel. Add pandanus leaves, cover tightly, then place steamer over a saucepan of simmering water and cook for 20 minutes or until rice is tender. Transfer to a bowl, discarding leaves. Using a large metal spoon, stir in coconut milk and a pinch of salt. Serve immediately.

Recipe

  • 450 g (2¼ cups) glutinous rice
  • 1 tsp ground turmeric
  • 3 pandanus leaves (see Note), bruised, tied in a knot
  • 125 ml (½ cup) coconut milk
