Hakka

Characteristics

Characteristics

Hakka food is famous for being salty, fragrant, and fatty.The saltiness is present due to the fact that in the past, workers tended to use salt to preserve their food. The salt also enables them to work for longer hours. The fattiness is present due to the need to provide the workers with enough energy for them to work harder and longer. Hakka cuisine usually include pickled and dry food so they can last longer. This is due to the fact that food used to be very scarce for the Hakka people, so when food comes, they will have to keep them for as long as possible.

Abacus Beads

It was called abacus beads because it looked like the seeds in an abacus. Abacus beads are made from yam paste and some types of starches, usually tapioca or wheat starch. It is a savoury dish that can be served as a main meal or as a snack. Traditionally, Hakkas eat Abacus beads every Chinese New Year.

Lei Cha

The custom of Lei cha began in the Three Kingdoms period or even Han Dynasty. It is very prevalent among Hakka people in Hakka regions of Taiwan. It is brought by Hakka people to Taiwan, Malaysia, and any locales with a substantial Hakka diaspora population. Besides Hakka Lei cha, Lei cha is also very traditional among Hunanese people in Northern part of Hunan Province of Mainland China. So, the Lei cha custom in China has two different kinds: Hakka Lei cha; and Hunan Lei cha.
