History

Bak-kut-teh is a herbal soup dish developed in Malaya among Hokkien immigrant communities. It is popularly thought to have originated in Klang, whereas the Teochew Variant is thought to have originated from the coolies working at the wharves along Singapore River. The dish is reported to supplement the meagre diet of port coolies and as a tonic to boost their health.[citation needed] The Teochews came later and the main visual difference between the Hokkien and Teochew version of bak kut teh is that the Hokkiens use dark soy sauce and thus the soup base is characteristically darker in colour


Instructions

1. Blanch the pork ribs in a pot of boiling water to remove the scum

2. Drain, cut into rib pieces

3. Leave aside in a bowl of cool water

4. Add garlic, onion, crushed white peppercorns, dang gui, codonopsis (and the 3 optional ingredients if youâ€™re using it) into a stock pot with 1.2L of water, bring to a boil.

5. Lower the rib pieces into the stock

6. Cover and simmer for 1 hour

7. 30 minutes before serving, add the goji berries

Recipe

  • 600g free range pork ribs
  • 1.2 litres water
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 bulb garlic, cloves separated but not peeled
  • 1 tablespoon soya sauce
  • Half a big red onion
  • 1/2 teaspoon white pepper
  • 3-5g crushed white peppercorns (depending on how peppery you want it to be)
  • 2-3 slices of dang gui
  • 2 sticks of codonopsis
  • 4g goji berries
  • minced garlic
  • Red chillies
  • Dark soya sauce
  • Long grain jasmine rice
  • Optional:
    2-3 pieces liquorice root
    Few slices of ligusticum
    1 small star anise
