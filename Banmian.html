Ban Mian

  1. Home
  2. Ban Mian

History

Ban Mian originated from Northern China. Ban Mian is a recent addition to the Singapore culinary scene. As the older generations of Singaporeans are all Southerners, the Ban Mian was not on their menu. The new wave immigrants from mainland China (especially Northern China) has brought along this delicious noodle variety. Ban Mian stalls are springing up all over Singapore, in hawker centers, as well as in restaurants.


Instructions

1. Place water in a large mixing bowl, gradually add in flour and sea salt and mix well.

2. When flour and water is mixed into a dough, create a well in the middle of the dough. Pour egg and oil into the well and slowly mix well and knead it till not sticky.

3. Place the dough in an airtight container or cover it with a cling film, and put aside for about 20 minutes.

4. Roll the dough into 2-3mm-thick sheet, hand-tear into bite-size pieces or cut into strands with a pasta maker.

5. Dust the Ban Mian in the extra flour to avoid being stuck into a chunk.

6. Fill up half a large stock pot with water, add in half the amount of anchovies and bring it to a boil. Reduce to low heat and simmer for another 1 hour. 7. Whilst the stock is brewing, deepfry the remaining anchovies till crispy. Strain and set aside.

8. Deepfry the onion till crsipy, strain and set aside.

9. n a medium stock pot, bring about half the pot of water to boil and cook the black fungus strips till soften. Strain and set aside. 10. Repeat step 7 with Chinese mushroom strips.

11. Rinse the sweet leaf bush and remove all the leaves into a bowl. Set aside.

12. Rinse the medium stock pot, fill it with 2 medium bowls of the anchovy stock. Add in the leaves of sweet leaf bush and bring to a boil. Then put in the prepared Ban Mian (whatever amount you wish to eat - usually a handful will feed one). Constantly stir the Ban Mian with a pair of wooden chopsticks to prevent it being stuck together.

13. When the noodle floats to the surface, turn heat off.

14. Divide the Ban Mian into 2 serving bowls and pour the soup over. Sprinkle over heaps of mushroom strips, black fungus strips, deepfried anchovies and deepfried onion. Add some freshly ground pepper and salt to taste.

Serve.

Recipe

  • 250 gram flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon sea salt
  • 1 egg
  • 60 ml water
  • 1 tablespoon sunflower oil
  • Some extra flour for dusting
  • A handful of dried Mu-Er (wood gungus/black fungus), soaked and cut into thin strips
  • 2-3 Chinese mushroom, soaked and cut into thin strips
  • A pinch of freshly ground pepper and salt
  • 1 bunch of sweet leaf bush (pucuk manis in Malay)
  • 1 large onion, thinly sliced
