History

It was found in China when an emperor ate congee with millet which then had many variations since then. There is Cantonese congee, Teochew congee, also known as Mui, and much more. Mui origin is unknown and is based on many historical accounts is served during times of famine in China. Congee was adapted by the Teochew and was changed in Chaoshan in eastern Guangdong to form their very own type of congee which is much more watery than Cantonese congee and is often eaten with youtiao.


Instructions

1.Combine rice and water in a large pot. Turn on the stove and bring water to a boil. Reduce heat to medium low and allow it to simmer for 15 mins. Remove lid to prevent over boiling. Turn off heat before serving.

Recipe

  • 1 and a half cups of rice (300g), rinsed and drained
  • 8 cups (1.9 litres) of water
  • Choice of side dishes:
  • Pickled mustard
  • Chili bamboo shoots
  • Spicy pickled radish
  • Canned dace
  • Fermented beancurd
